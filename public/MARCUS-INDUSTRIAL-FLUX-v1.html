DIAGNOSTIC: Forklifts Don't Break Floors. Wrong Specifications Do.

Impact Failure: Dropping a 5,000 lb pallet from 2 feet = 20,000 ft-lbs of energy. Standard epoxy? Cracks. Premium epoxy? Absorbs it.
Point Loading: Forklift wheels concentrate 8,000 lbs on 2 square inches. That's 4,000 PSI. Concrete fails. Our coating handles it.
Chemical Attack: Manufacturing spills aren't just water. Solvents, acids, bases. One wrong chemical and your floor dissolves.
Thermal Shock: Hot metal welding + cold floor + water quench = concrete cracks. Epoxy cracks right with it unless designed for it.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
Industrial floors don't "wear out" - they fail from impact or chemical attack. The only variables: impact resistance (measured in ft-lbs) and chemical resistance (measured in classes). Everything else is marketing. Premium tier gets you 140 ft-lbs impact resistance and Class 1 chemical resistance. Essential tier gets 50 ft-lbs and Class 3. For a warehouse running forklifts? The choice is clear.

INTERNAL - Industrial Flooring Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose the Duty Level

  • "What's your maximum forklift capacity?" (Under 5K lbs = Professional. Over 10K lbs = Premium. Over 20K lbs = FluxCore minimum.)
  • "How often do you drop pallets?" (Never = Professional. Occasionally = Premium. Regularly = FluxCore.)
  • "What chemicals do you use?" (Just oil/water = Professional. Solvents/acids = Premium. Battery acid/strong solvents = FluxCore.)
  • "What's your daily revenue if production stops?" (Under $5K = Professional. $5-20K = Premium. Over $20K = FluxCore.)
  • "Do you have thermal cycling?" (Hot welding + cold floor = Premium minimum. Concrete cracks, coating cracks unless designed for it.)
FLUX TIP
Industrial buyers care about one thing: uptime. If a floor failure shuts down a $50M/year facility for 3 days, that's $600K in lost revenue. Our Premium tier costs $9-13/sq ft. For a 20,000 sq ft warehouse, that's $180-260K. That's 30-40% of ONE shutdown cost. The ROI calculation writes itself.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "Light duty, basic protection. For storage areas, light forklifts, low-impact environments. If you're not dropping heavy pallets, this works. 5-year warranty."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "Standard industrial duty. Good impact and chemical resistance. For standard manufacturing and warehousing. Most facilities choose this tier. 10-year warranty."
  • PREMIUM: "Heavy industrial. Maximum impact and chemical resistance. For facilities dropping pallets daily or working with aggressive chemicals. 15-year warranty."
  • FLUX-CORE: "Extreme duty, 2-day installation, 20-year warranty. For 24/7 facilities where any downtime costs more than the floor. Install Friday, run Monday."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"We just seal the concrete"

Concrete sealers don't provide impact resistance. Dropping a pallet on sealed concrete? The concrete cracks. The sealer cracks right with it. Epoxy provides a continuous film that absorbs impact energy. Sealers protect against stains. Epoxy protects against damage.

"We replace the floor every 10 years anyway"

Concrete replacement costs $15-25/sq ft. Our Professional tier is $5-8/sq ft. For a 20,000 sq ft warehouse: replacement = $300-500K. Professional epoxy = $100-160K. Plus: 10 years of maintenance-free operation vs yearly sealing. The math says epoxy wins.

"How long until we can run forklifts?"

Professional: 72 hours full cure. Light traffic at 24, but heavy forklifts need 72. Premium: 72 hours. FluxCore: 24 hours. If you absolutely cannot wait 3 days, FluxCore is the only option. It costs more, but if you're losing $20K/day, it pays for itself in 2 days.

"What about expansion joints?"

We leave existing expansion joints open but fill them with flexible epoxy. The floor moves with the concrete without cracking. Structural joints stay open. Control joints can be filled if you want. We don't hide expansion joints - we accommodate them properly.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most industrial facilities go with Professional - it's the right balance of durability and cost. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you're dropping pallets regularly or using aggressive chemicals, I'd strongly recommend Premium. One dropped pallet can pay for the upgrade."
  • "Either way, get the moisture test done. Even if you don't use us, you need to know what's coming through your slab."
