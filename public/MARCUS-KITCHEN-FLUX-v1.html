DIAGNOSTIC: Health Inspectors Don't Fail Floors. They Fail Grout Lines.

Grout Harbors Bacteria: Every cracked grout line is a biofilm factory. Salmonella, E. coli, Listeria. Health inspectors don't even need to test - they see cracks and write it up.
Porous Tile: Unsealed tile absorbs spills. Grease in the pores? You can clean it, but it's still there. Next inspector with a blacklight? Failed.
Drain Traps: Tile drains have nooks and crannies. Food collects. Bacteria grows. Mopping pushes it into the cracks. Cleaning just spreads it around.
Slip Hazard: Wet tile + greasy kitchen = slip and fall lawsuit. OSHA and health departments both cite it. Average settlement: $150K.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
I've watched restaurants shut down for grout line failures while the epoxy floor across the aisle passed. Health inspectors don't care about your culinary credentials - they care about crevices. If it's seamless, they move on. If it has cracks, they write it up. Every time. The floor you can clean is the floor that passes inspections.
