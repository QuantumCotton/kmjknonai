The 20-Second Pitch (verbatim)
“For $14,900/mo (Standard), we handle facilities & landscaping to keep you stable. It allows for slow improvements, but maintenance comes first.

For $19,200/mo (Premium), we shift gears. We bring in extra capacity to handle the daily grind, freeing us up to run a Phased Renovation Plan.

First 90 days, we stabilize the property. Then, we use the pool to renovate units phase-by-phase. Your budget never changes—if we have a light maintenance week, we renovate faster. If it’s heavy, we slow down. You get a predictable bill while we modernize your asset.”
The "Stabilization" Strategy
  • Months 1-3: We fix the critical issues and learn the building.
  • Month 4+: We set a "Renovation Velocity" based on the stabilized pool.
  • Variable Speed: Good weeks = more renovation. Bad weeks = we handle the crisis.
  • Fixed Cost: The owner pays the same flat rate regardless of speed.
Objections + clean replies
Owner: “Can you renovate a unit a month?” Reply: “Maybe, but we won’t promise that until after the 90-day stabilization. We’ll phase it in. The goal is consistent progress without blowing the budget.”
Owner: “Why not just stick with Standard?” Reply: “You can. But Standard is mostly reactive. You’ll get renovations done, but much slower. Premium buys the manpower to actually focus on upgrades.”
Owner: “I don’t want surprise costs.” Reply: “That’s why you pay the flat subscription. If renovations take longer because of maintenance issues, you don’t pay extra—it just takes a bit more time.”
Owner: “Why separate turnovers?” Reply: “Turns are vacancy-driven and volatile. Flat-rate turns keep it fair and transparent.”
Owner: “What about storms?” Reply: “Storm services are optional and priced clearly—prep, shutters, and post-storm assessment.”
Recommended pricing logic (why $14,900 is fair for “mid condition”)
This is the “middle mediocrity” capacity call:
  • Without records, professional underwriting for vintage FL multifamily usually lands around 30–45 hrs/week to stay stable.
  • 32 hrs/week is a conservative-but-not-worst-case starting point that:
    • covers ongoing tickets,
    • keeps preventive work happening,
    • and still avoids the “full-time tech” pricing shock of 60 hrs/week.
  • Premium (60 hrs/week) is the "Growth" engine. It allows us to offload basic tasks and focus hours on the renovations you actually want.
Optional tiering (if you want it)
Budget (Lean) 24 hrs/week → $8,100/month (Reactive Only)
Standard (Recommended) 32 hrs/week → $14,900/month (Full Coverage)
Premium (Growth) 60 hrs/week → $19,200/month (Renovation Subscription)
What not to say
  • Don’t promise rollover credits
  • Don’t say “unlimited” anything
  • Don’t blend renovation into maintenance without approval
  • Don’t negotiate hourly rates—adjust weekly pool instead
