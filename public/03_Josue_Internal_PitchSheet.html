The 20-Second Pitch (verbatim)
“For $10,580 a month, we run a pooled facilities labor program across all 22 units, plus full landscaping. It’s 32 hours a week dedicated to this portfolio, used flexibly for repairs, prevention, and small improvements. You get one bill, fewer emergencies over time, and the ability to stay ahead instead of falling behind.”
How the pool works (don’t mess this up)
  • One shared pool. Not “free hours.”
  • Heavy repair month → hours shift to repairs
  • Light month → hours go to preventive + improvements
  • No rollovers. No credits.
  • This is how we absorb bad months without surprise bills.
Objections + clean replies
Owner: “That seems high.” Reply: “It includes a weekly dedicated capacity. This isn’t random dispatch; it’s facilities coverage plus landscaping, with predictable cost.”
Owner: “Can we lower the price?” Reply: “Yes—by choosing a smaller weekly hour pool. Price tracks capacity.”
Owner: “I don’t want surprise costs.” Reply: “That’s the point of the pool. Overages are only if we exceed the weekly hours, and rates are published up front.”
Owner: “Why separate turnovers?” Reply: “Turns are vacancy-driven and volatile. Flat-rate turns keep it fair and transparent.”
Owner: “What about storms?” Reply: “Storm services are optional and priced clearly—prep, shutters, and post-storm assessment.”
Recommended pricing logic (why $10,580 is fair for “mid condition”)
This is the “middle mediocrity” capacity call:
  • Without records, professional underwriting for vintage FL multifamily usually lands around 30–45 hrs/week to stay stable.
  • 32 hrs/week is a conservative-but-not-worst-case starting point that:
    • covers ongoing tickets,
    • keeps preventive work happening,
    • and still avoids the “full-time tech” pricing shock of 60 hrs/week.
  • Budget (24 hrs/week) is for lighter portfolios or owners comfortable with more reactive maintenance.
  • Premium (60 hrs/week) provides near full-time coverage for high-touch portfolios or aggressive preventive programs.
Optional tiering (if you want it)
Budget (Lean) 24 hrs/week → $8,100/month (same structure, less capacity)
Standard (Recommended) 32 hrs/week → $10,580/month
Premium (Full Coverage) 60 hrs/week → $19,200/month (closest to “on-call facilities team”)
What not to say
  • Don’t promise rollover credits
  • Don’t say “unlimited” anything
  • Don’t blend renovation into maintenance without approval
  • Don’t negotiate hourly rates—adjust weekly pool instead
