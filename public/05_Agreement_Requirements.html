Agreement Requirements — Air-Tight Provisions

Legal Mechanics for the Pooled Labor Model
Internal Drafting Guide

1. The "Labor Pool" Mechanism (Core Risk)

"Use-It-or-Lose-It" Clause
  • Explicitly state that unused Weekly Pool Hours expire at 11:59 PM on Sunday.
  • Prohibited: Rollover credits, cash value redemption, or "banking" hours.
  • Rationale: Prevents paying for 3 quiet months then demanding 100 hours in Week 13.
Definition of "Hour"
  • Clock Start: Upon arrival at the portfolio/site (Portal-to-Portal if negotiated).
  • Minimum Increments: 30-minute blocks.
  • Trip Charges: Materials sourcing time counts against the pool.
Overage Mechanics
  • Trigger: Hours > 32 (Standard) or > 60 (Premium).
  • Rate: $78/hr (Standard) / $117/hr (After-hours).
  • Notification: KMJK notifies when pool is exhausted; work pauses until Overage authorized.

2. Scope: Maintenance vs. Renovation

"Minor Repair" (Included - All Tiers)
  • Repairs intended to restore functionality to an existing asset.
  • Cap: Any single task > 4 hours continuous labor = Project/Renovation (requires separate scope).
  • Exclusions: Flooring (>1 room), full interior paint, cabinets.
"Renovation" (Excluded from Standard/Budget)
  • Improvements intended to increase asset value or change aesthetics.
  • Trigger: Requires separate approval of scope and budget.
★ Premium Tier Exception ("Renovation Subscription")
  • Authorized Use: Premium Tier clients are authorized to use Pool Hours for Capital Improvements.
  • Phase 1 (Stabilization): Months 1-3 focus on critical repairs. Renovation timelines are estimated, not guaranteed.
  • Phase 2 (Roadmap): "Renovation Roadmap" agreed monthly. Unused maintenance hours shift to Roadmap projects (Variable Velocity).

3. Turnover Pricing Protection

"Standard Turn" ($1,200)
  • Includes: Patching nail holes (<20), spot paint (<20%), deep clean, safety check.
  • Condition Precedent: Unit must be "broom clean" and free of personal property.
"Heavy Turn" Triggers (Billable/Quoted)
  • Trash: > 2 kitchen bags of debris left behind.
  • Drywall: Holes > 4 inches diameter.
  • Paint: Full wall color change or nicotine/smoke remediation.
  • Biohazard: Pet waste, infestation (automatic exclusion).

4. Waivers & Liability

Storm Prep (Effort vs. Outcome)
  • "Owner acknowledges Storm Prep Services constitute a best-effort service. KMJK does not guarantee prevention of damage. KMJK is not an insurer."
Declined Services (Add-On Menu)
  • HVAC Waiver: If declined, Owner accepts liability for accelerated corrosion/coil failure.
  • Sewer Waiver: If declined, Owner accepts liability for main line stoppages.

5. Material Markup & Spending

  • Emergency Authority: KMJK may spend up to $1,000 without prior approval strictly to stop active damage.
  • Non-Emergency Cap: Strict $250 limit without written approval.
  • Markup: Cost + 20%. Sourcing labor deducted from Labor Pool.

6. Anti-Gaming & Fees

The "Tier Switcher" Trap
  • Downgrade Penalty: Downgrading from Premium to Budget triggers a "Condition Reset" inspection fee to prevent fixing neglect on Premium hours then dropping.
  • Termination: 30-day notice required.
One-Page Close Compatibility
  • The Proposal is the Term Sheet.
  • It references the Master Service Agreement (MSA) at a fixed URL.
  • Signature agrees to the "Use-It-or-Lose-It" policy.
