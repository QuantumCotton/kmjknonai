Article 1: The Service Model (Capacity Access)

1.1. Nature of Service

Client acknowledges that this Agreement is a subscription for access to pooled facilities capacity, not a traditional time-and-materials labor contract. The Monthly Fee guarantees that specific labor hours ("Pool Hours") are reserved exclusively for the Client's portfolio each week.

1.2. The "Use-It-or-Lose-It" Rule

Pool Hours are provisioned on a weekly cycle (Monday–Sunday). Unused Pool Hours expire automatically at 11:59 PM each Sunday. Unused hours do not roll over, do not accrue, and have no cash redemption value. This structure ensures resources are available when needed without creating future liability for either party.

1.3. Definition of "Pool Hour"

Hours are billed based on technician availability allocated to the Portfolio ("Portal-to-Portal"). Time spent sourcing materials, coordinating vendors for the property, and traveling between portfolio sites is counted against the Pool.

Article 2: Permitted Use & Tiers

2.1. Standard Tier (Maintenance Only)

For Clients on the Standard Tier, Pool Hours are strictly limited to "Repair, Restoration, and Prevention."

  • Permitted: Leak repairs, electrical fixes, filter changes, safety checks, aesthetic touch-ups.
  • Prohibited: Capital improvements, full room painting, flooring replacement, remodeling.
  • Rationale: Standard capacity (32 hrs) is calibrated to maintain the asset, not to upgrade it.

2.2. Premium Tier ("Renovation Subscription")

For Clients on the Premium Tier, Pool Hours are authorized for "Growth & Renovation."

  • Phase 1: Stabilization (Months 1-3): Provider will utilize Pool Hours to address critical maintenance, learn building systems, and stabilize the asset. Renovation timelines will be estimated but not guaranteed during this period.
  • Phase 2: Renovation Roadmap: After Month 3, Parties will agree on a "Phased Renovation Plan." Provider will allocate available Pool Hours to these projects.
  • Variable Velocity: Client acknowledges that renovation speed is dependent on maintenance volume. Light maintenance weeks will accelerate renovation; heavy maintenance weeks will slow it. The Monthly Fee remains fixed regardless of velocity.
  • Materials: Labor is covered by the subscription; renovation materials are billed separately per Article 3.3.

Article 3: Financial Terms

3.1. Monthly Fee & Billing

Service is billed flat-rate monthly in advance. Payment is due on the 1st of the service month.

3.2. Overages

Work requested by Client that exceeds the weekly Pool limit will be billed at the Overage Rate specified in the Proposal ($78/hr Standard, $117/hr After-Hours). Provider will notify Client when the Pool is 90% exhausted; work will pause until Overage is authorized.

3.3. Materials & Expenses

Materials are billed separately at Cost + 20%. Provider is authorized to purchase materials up to $250 per incident without prior approval. Emergency mitigation (active water/fire risk) has an automatic approval limit of $1,000 to prevent asset damage.

Article 4: Exclusions & Special Services

4.1. Turnovers (Make-Ready)

Unit Turnovers are excluded from the Labor Pool and billed at the fixed rates listed in the Proposal ($1,200 Standard Turn). This separation ensures that high-vacancy months do not consume the maintenance capacity of the occupied units.

4.2. Storm & Emergency Waivers

Acknowledgement of Risk: Provider's "Storm Prep" service is a best-effort service. Provider guarantees the deployment of resources (closing shutters, securing items) but does not guarantee the safety of the property or the prevention of damage. Provider is not an insurer.

4.3. Declined Services

If Client declines specific preventive add-ons (e.g., HVAC Drain Line Service, Sewer Jetting), Client accepts full liability for failures related to those systems (e.g., water damage from backed-up AC lines).

Article 5: Term & Termination

5.1. Notice Period

Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days' written notice.

5.2. Anti-Gaming (Tier Switching)

Client acknowledges that downgrading from Premium to Standard forfeits the "Renovation Subscription." If Client subsequently requests to re-upgrade to Premium within 6 months, a "Condition Reset Inspection" ($500) will be required to ensure Premium hours are not used to remedy neglect accumulated during the Standard period.
