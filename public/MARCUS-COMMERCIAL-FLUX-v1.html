INTERNAL - Commercial Flooring Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose the Pain Point

  • "What's the biggest problem with your current floor?" (Cracks? Stains? Downtime? Liability?)
  • "How many people walk through this area daily?" (Foot traffic tier = durability tier)
  • "What's your business continuity risk if this floor fails?" (One shutdown = Professional tier minimum)
  • "What's your 5-year floor maintenance budget?" (Coated floor ROI calculation time)
  • "Have you had liability issues from floor conditions?" (Slip hazards = Professional tier, full disclosure needed)
FLUX TIP
Commercial buyers don't care about pretty floors. They care about P&L. If you can show them that spending $50K on flooring saves $300K over 10 years, the sale is already made. Quantify the problem, sell the solution. Don't sell epoxy - sell ROI.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "Dust control, easy cleaning, 5-year warranty. For back offices, storage areas, anywhere foot traffic is light. If it's not customer-facing and not revenue-generating, this is the right choice."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "Customer-facing areas. Spills wipe up with one pass. UV protection keeps colors bright. 10-year warranty. This is what retailers, restaurants, and office lobbies choose."
  • PREMIUM: "For heavy traffic - warehouses, manufacturing, loading docks. Forklift rated, chemical resistant, impact resistant. This floor doesn't crack when you drop a pallet. 15-year warranty."
  • FLUX-CORE: "The last floor you'll ever buy. Extreme durability, 2-day installation, 20-year warranty. For facilities where downtime costs more than the floor. One installation, done forever."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"That's a lot per square foot"

Let's run the 10-year numbers. Uncosted concrete: $150-250K in replacements. Coated floor: $50-120K. Difference: $100-130K. Cleaning labor difference: $15-30K. Liability risk reduction: Priceless. You're not spending money - you're avoiding loss.

"We can shut down for repairs, it's cheaper"

One shutdown costs $5-50K in lost revenue. Do that 5 times in 10 years and you're at $25-250K - plus repair costs. Our Professional tier costs $50-70K for 10,000 sq ft. Do the math. Proactive vs reactive isn't a choice.

"How long will we be shut down?"

Essential: 2-3 days. Professional: 3-4 days. Premium: 3-4 days. FluxCore: 2 days. We can work weekends, nights, or in sections if you can't fully shut down. Premium tiers worth 1 extra day for 5 extra years of warranty.

"What if it cracks?"

We use 2-part crack isolation primers. Hairline cracks in the substrate? The coating bridges them. Structural movement? We use flexible membranes. Our warranty covers delamination and coating failure. If it's a structural slab issue, that's a building problem, not a flooring problem - but we'll identify it before quoting.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most commercial facilities go with Professional - it's the right balance of cost and durability. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you want to see ROI projections, I can run a 10-year cost analysis for your facility."
  • "Either way, get the moisture test done. Even if you don't use us, you need to know what's coming through your slab."
