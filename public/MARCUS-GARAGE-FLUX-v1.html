DIAGNOSTIC: Garage Floor Failure Modes

Concrete Dust: Porous concrete releases silica dust that coats everything - tools, cars, enters living space
Oil Staining: Concrete absorbs hydrocarbons permanently. No amount of cleaning removes deep penetration
Moisture Migration: Florida humidity + no vapor barrier = moisture pushing coatings off from below
Hot Tire Pickup: Standard epoxy softens at 140°F. Tires coming off hot asphalt? 180°F+.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
90% of "epoxy failures" aren't epoxy failures. They're moisture failures. If you don't test for MVT (moisture vapor transmission) before quoting, you're gambling, not installing. I've seen $15K garage floors peel in 30 days because someone skipped a $15 calcium chloride test.

INTERNAL - Garage Flooring Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose Before You Prescribe

  • "What's the biggest problem with your current floor?" (Dust, stains, cracks, looks bad?)
  • "How old is the concrete?" (New concrete needs 28+ days cure before coating)
  • "Do you park daily drivers or classics?" (Hot tires require Professional tier or above)
  • "Does the garage door stay open?" (UV exposure requires HALS protection)
  • "Any previous coatings?" (Failed coatings must be ground off - add $1/sq ft for prep)
FLUX TIP
If they say "the floor is fine, I just want it to look better" - they're Essential tier. If they say "I'm tired of concrete dust in my house" - they're Professional tier. If they say "I want a showroom floor for my car collection" - they're Premium. Let their problem sell the tier.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "This seals the concrete and stops the dust. Not fancy, but it works. 5-year warranty."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "This is what most homeowners choose. UV protection, hot tire rated, decorative flake if you want it. Looks like a $100K garage for $3K."
  • PREMIUM: "You're not just coating a floor - you're building a showroom. Metallic finish, 15-year warranty, this is the last floor you'll ever need."
  • FLUX-CORE: "Our proprietary system. Installed today, drive on tonight. 20-year warranty. When downtime isn't an option."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"That's more than I expected"

Compare to what they'd spend on flooring that lasts: Hardwood ($12-20/sq ft), quality tile ($8-15/sq ft), polished concrete ($8-12/sq ft). Our Professional tier is $5-7/sq ft and lasts 10+ years with zero maintenance. Plus: it seals the concrete forever. No more dust, no more stains.

"I can get it cheaper at Home Depot"

You can. Rust-Oleum kit is $150 for a 2-car garage. Here's what you get: 50% water content (so half your film thickness evaporates), no UV protection (it'll yellow in 6 months), no hot tire rating (it'll lift where your tires sit), and zero warranty when it fails. Our Professional tier is 100% solids, UV protected, hot tire rated, 10-year warranty. Different product, different result.

"How long until I can park on it?"

Professional and Premium tiers: 72 hours for full cure. You can walk on it in 24 hours, but tires need the full cure. If you can't wait 3 days, our FluxCore system is drive-on in 4 hours. Costs more, but if you don't have alternative parking, it's worth it.

"What if it peels?"

Peeling happens for one reason: moisture. We test for moisture vapor transmission before we quote. If your concrete has high MVT, we use a vapor-blocking primer. We don't skip steps. That's why our floors don't peel.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most people go with Professional - it's the sweet spot of performance and price. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you want to see it before you commit, I can show you samples of all four tiers."
  • "Either way, get the moisture test done. Even if you don't use us, you need to know what's coming through your slab."
FLUX CLOSING TECHNIQUE
Never ask "Which one do you want?" Ask "What's the biggest problem with your current floor?" Then solve that problem. If they say dust, prescribe Professional. If they say "I want it to look amazing," prescribe Premium. Don't sell tiers - sell solutions. The tier is just the vehicle.
