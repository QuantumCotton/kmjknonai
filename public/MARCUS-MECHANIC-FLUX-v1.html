DIAGNOSTIC: Oil Is Eating Your Concrete

Deep Penetration: Motor oil doesn't sit on top - it soaks in 1-4 inches. You can't clean it. You can't seal it. It's there forever.
Surface Degradation: Oil breaks down concrete binder. Your slab gets soft, powdery, crumbles under pressure. Good luck with that hoist.
Stain Migration: That stain? It keeps growing. 6 months later, it's 3x larger. New oil spreads along the oil-soaked channels.
Coating Failure: Epoxy doesn't bond to oil. You coat over oil-stained concrete? It peels in 3-6 months. Every time.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
In 20 years of floors, I've seen exactly TWO properly prepped mechanic floors fail. Both were oil-contaminated slabs that weren't degreased. You can't clean oil with degreaser once it's in the concrete - you need to grind it out. 1/4 inch minimum. Anything less is a warranty claim waiting to happen.

INTERNAL - Mechanic Shop Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose the Oil Situation

  • "How long has this floor been exposed to oil?" (5 years = contaminated. 20 years = ruined. Both need aggressive prep.)
  • "Any oil stains visible now?" (Each stain needs spot grinding. Total prep time estimate goes up.)
  • "What kind of work do you do?" (Oil changes = Professional. Engine rebuilds = Premium. Transmission = Premium minimum.)
  • "Do you have hoists or are you on slab?" (Hoists need anchor holes filled + reinforcement. Add prep cost.)
  • "How many bays and how busy are you?" (Downtime calculation. 4-bay shop = $2K/day revenue lost. FluxCore pays for itself fast.)
FLUX TIP
Never quote a mechanic shop without seeing the oil damage first. Walk the floor. Press on soft spots. Check for stains. If they say "it's not that bad," show them the concrete dust when you press it. That's not dust - that's disintegrating concrete. That's the reason for the quote.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "Basic oil resistance, easy cleanup. If you're doing light repair and don't spill a lot, this is the right choice. 5-year warranty."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "Good chemical resistance, standard anti-slip. For general repair shops working with oil, brake fluid, and gas. Most shops choose this tier. 10-year warranty."
  • PREMIUM: "For shops working with transmission fluid, solvents, brake cleaner. Novolac formulation is chemically impermeable. Nothing soaks in. Nothing stains. 15-year warranty."
  • FLUX-CORE: "Our proprietary system. 2-day installation, maximum durability, 20-year warranty. For shops that can't afford downtime or want the absolute best."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"I can get epoxy from an auto parts store"

You can. But here's the problem: auto parts epoxy is designed for DIY garages. Thin film (50 mil vs our 125 mil). No oil containment. It'll peel right where your oil stains are. Plus: no warranty. When it fails, you're doing it again. Our Professional tier is 3x thicker, 10-year warranty, guaranteed not to peel on properly prepped concrete.

"The floor is already stained, doesn't matter what I put down"

Wrong. Coating over stained concrete = guaranteed failure. The oil is still in the slab, breaking it down. We grind 1/4-1/2 inch minimum. That's expensive, but it's the only way to get a floor that lasts. You can either do the work now, or do it again in 6 months.

"How long until I can drive on it?"

Professional: 72 hours full cure. You can walk on it in 24, but jacks and hoists need the full 72. Premium: 72 hours. FluxCore: 24 hours. If you can't wait 3 days, FluxCore is the only choice. It costs more, but if you're losing $500+/day, it pays for itself.

"What about the hoist anchors?"

We fill all anchor holes with epoxy grout, then reinforce them. You can bolt your hoists right back on. Some shops ask us to leave anchor holes open and just patch around them - we don't recommend it. The epoxy protects the concrete everywhere or it doesn't protect it at all.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most mechanic shops go with Professional - it's the right balance of cost and protection. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you work with transmission fluid or solvents, I'd strongly recommend Premium. Chemical resistance you don't think you need until you need it."
  • "Either way, we need to grind out the oil contamination. That's non-negotiable. But once it's done, the floor is sealed forever."
