DIAGNOSTIC: Skydrol Dissolves Standard Epoxy. We Don't Use Standard Epoxy.

Skydrol Attack: Skydrol (hydraulic fluid) attacks polyester and polyamide epoxies. It softens them, bubbles them, dissolves them. 24-48 hours and your floor is gone.
Jet Fuel Spills: Jet-A and Jet-B are aggressive solvents. Standard epoxy? Softens and delaminates. Novolac? Impermeable. Nothing soaks in.
Tire Heat: Aircraft tires reach 200°F+. Standard epoxy softens at 140°F. Premium handles 250°F. FluxCore handles 350°F.
Deicing Fluids: Propylene glycol and glycerin-based deicers contain chemicals that attack standard coatings. One application and the coating fails.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
I've seen hangar floors delaminate after one Skydrol spill. The mechanic mops it up, but the damage is done. Skydrol penetrated the epoxy, attacked the bond line, and the coating peeled off in sheets. That's a $50K floor replacement. Premium tier with Novolac doesn't have this problem. Skydrol doesn't dissolve Novolac. It just sits there until you wipe it up.

INTERNAL - Aircraft Hangar Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose the Aircraft Exposure

  • "What aircraft are stored/maintained here?" (General aviation = Professional. Business jets = Premium. Commercial = Premium or FluxCore.)
  • "How much Skydrol and jet fuel exposure do you expect?" (Occasional spills = Professional. Regular maintenance = Premium. Heavy = FluxCore.)
  • "Do you work with deicing fluids on the floor?" (Yes = Premium minimum. Glycol-based deicers attack standard coatings.)
  • "What's the aircraft tire load?" (Cessna = Professional. Business jets = Premium. Commercial aircraft = FluxCore.)
  • "How much does it cost to relocate an aircraft for floor work?" (If it's more than the floor cost, FluxCore 2-day install pays for itself.)
FLUX TIP
Hangar floors don't just "get coated" - they get certified. FAA and military specs require Skydrol resistance. Standard epoxy doesn't meet spec. Novolac does. If you're coating a hangar with anything less than Novolac, you're not just risking failure - you're risking non-compliance.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "Basic protection for general aviation storage. No aircraft chemicals. If you're not dealing with Skydrol or jet fuel on the floor, this works. 5-year warranty."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "For light aircraft maintenance. Good chemical resistance, occasional Skydrol exposure. Most GA hangars choose this tier. 10-year warranty."
  • PREMIUM: "For business jets and commercial aircraft. Novolac formulation is chemically impermeable to Skydrol and jet fuel. 15-year warranty. FAA compliant."
  • FLUX-CORE: "For military and commercial fleet hangars. 2-day installation, 20-year warranty, maximum chemical resistance. When aircraft downtime costs more than the floor, this is the choice."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"We just mop up spills immediately"

That doesn't stop Skydrol. Skydrol penetrates in minutes, not hours. By the time you grab the mop, it's already attacking the epoxy. Premium tier with Novolac resists Skydrol completely - it sits on the surface until you wipe it up. No penetration, no damage.

"That's more than standard epoxy"

Standard epoxy costs less, but it also dissolves on contact with Skydrol. One Skydrol spill = $10-50K floor replacement. Our Premium tier costs $11-15/sq ft. For a 5,000 sq ft hangar, that's $55-75K. One Skydrol spill costs more than the entire floor. That's not pricing - that's insurance.

"How long until we can bring aircraft back?"

Professional: 72 hours full cure. Light aircraft at 24, but heavy aircraft need 72. Premium: 72 hours. FluxCore: 24 hours. If you absolutely cannot have aircraft out for 3 days, FluxCore is the only option.

"What about the expansion joints?"

Hangar floors have structural expansion joints every 20-30 feet. We leave these open but fill them with flexible epoxy. The floor moves with the concrete without cracking. FAA requires we don't seal structural joints. We comply.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most GA hangars go with Professional - it's the right balance for light aircraft work. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you're dealing with business jets or commercial aircraft, I'd strongly recommend Premium. Skydrol resistance isn't optional in that environment."
  • "Either way, get the moisture test done. Even if you don't use us, you need to know what's coming through your slab."
