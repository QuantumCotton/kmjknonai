DIAGNOSTIC: Your Floor Is The First Thing Customers Photograph. Make It Count.

First Impression: 93% of purchase decisions are visual. A stained, cracked floor signals "we don't care." A showroom floor signals "we're the best."
Social Sharing: Your showroom isn't just for customers - it's for Instagram. Every customer with a phone becomes a brand ambassador or brand critic.
Light Reflection: Polished concrete reflects 15% of light. High-gloss epoxy reflects 85%. Same showroom, 5x brighter, 5x more impressive.
Color Psychology: Floor color affects mood. Warm tones = comfort. Cool tones = sophistication. Dark tones = luxury. Wrong floor = wrong brand.
FLUX FIELD NOTE
I've watched customers walk into a showroom, look at the floor, and walk out. Not the cars, not the products - the floor. A dirty, worn floor says "we're cutting corners." A pristine, glossy floor says "we're the premium choice." Luxury buyers don't ask about price - they ask about quality. Your floor answers that question before you say a word.

INTERNAL - Luxury Showroom Sales Protocol

Step 1: Diagnose the Brand Value

  • "What's your average ticket price?" (Under $1K = Professional. $1-10K = Premium. Over $10K = FluxCore minimum. The floor needs to match the product.)
  • "What's your brand positioning?" (Value = Professional. Quality = Premium. Luxury = FluxCore. Discount brands don't need $20/sq ft floors.)
  • "Do customers take photos in the showroom?" (No = Professional. Yes = Premium. Yes + tag your brand = FluxCore. Instagram = showroom.)
  • "How important is floor maintenance to your daily operations?" (Can't clean during business hours = Premium or FluxCore. Non-yellowing is non-negotiable.)
  • "What's your ROI on brand differentiation?" (If 5% better branding = 10% more sales, the floor pays for itself.)
FLUX TIP
Luxury buyers aren't price-sensitive - they're quality-sensitive. A $100K car buyer notices a $20K floor. A $5K car buyer doesn't. Price the floor to match the product. Selling Ferraris on a $5/sq ft floor? Mixed message. Selling Hondas on a $20/sq ft floor? Overpriced. The floor must match the brand.

Step 2: Tier-Specific Pitch

  • ESSENTIAL: "Matte finish, solid colors. For back office, storage, non-customer-facing areas. Easy maintenance, clean appearance. 5-year warranty."
  • PROFESSIONAL: "Satin finish, decorative flakes. Clean, professional appearance that enhances the product without overshadowing it. Most retail showrooms choose this tier. 10-year warranty."
  • PREMIUM: "High-gloss metallic or marble finishes. The floor becomes part of the brand. Maximum UV protection, scratch resistance. For luxury brands where appearance is everything. 15-year warranty."
  • FLUX-CORE: "Ultra-gloss, unlimited custom colors, 2-day installation. For flagship showrooms where the floor IS the experience. 20-year warranty. The floor you build your brand around."

Step 3: Objection Protocol

"That's a lot for a floor"

Let's look at your average ticket. If you're selling $50K cars, what's 0.4% of that price? That's $200/sq ft. Our Premium tier is $13-20/sq ft. You're spending 0.2-0.3% of your product value on the floor that presents it. If the floor helps you close 2% more deals, it pays for itself in 2 months.

"Polished concrete is cheaper"

Polished concrete costs $5-8/sq ft. But it reflects 15% of light. Our Premium high-gloss reflects 92%. That's 6x brighter showroom for 2x the cost. Plus: polished concrete stains, scratches, and needs monthly polishing. Premium epoxy? Wipe and go. 5-year maintenance difference: $15-30K.

"The yellowing will show"

Standard epoxy yellows. Premium with FluxShield UV doesn't. That's 3% HALS and 1% UVA additives - UV absorbers and light stabilizers. Your floor looks the same in year 10 as it does in year 1. Non-yellowing isn't optional for luxury brands.

"How long until we can open?"

Professional: 72 hours full cure. Premium: 72 hours. FluxCore: 24 hours. If you absolutely cannot close for 3 days, FluxCore is the only option. 2-day installation costs more, but if you're losing $10K/day, it pays for itself.

Step 4: Close

  • "Most retail showrooms go with Professional - clean, professional, enhances the product without overwhelming it. Want me to write that up?"
  • "If you're a luxury brand or your customers take photos, I'd strongly recommend Premium. The floor is part of the brand experience."
  • "Either way, get the moisture test done. Even if you don't use us, you need to know what's coming through your slab."
